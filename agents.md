# Repository Goal

Create a practical framework for citizenship, representation, public trust, lawmaking, policy, taxation, budgeting, administration, transparency, corruption resistance, elections, public goods, emergency powers, federal scale, foreign policy, technology, and constrained political power downstream of Ethosism. The Governance Framework applies objective reality, reciprocity, integrity, long-term responsibility, and contribution to public decision-making.

# Writing Direction

When writing or revising this book, follow [docs/writing-guide.md](docs/writing-guide.md). Keep the Governance Framework grounded in observable reality, reciprocal moral reasoning, institutional constraint, public trust, competence, transparency, accountability, subsidiarity, rights, duties, tradeoffs, and long-term inheritance.

The work should be secular and non-theological: it may draw respectfully from religious, philosophical, civic, constitutional, historical, legal, economic, administrative, and practical traditions, but it must not depend on revelation, clergy, supernatural claims, partisan identity, technocratic authority, revolutionary romance, or any single theology for its authority.

Prefer concrete, defensible claims over vague civic language. Claims about governance should be testable against actual consequences, role reversal, public authority, incentives, budgets, administrative capacity, rights, minority protection, transparency, corruption risk, and decade-scale institutional responsibility.

The repo-local Codex skill for this work lives at [.agents/skills/governance-writing](.agents/skills/governance-writing). Keep Governance-specific Codex skills in this repository rather than installing them globally.

# Review Workflow

When reviewing or revising a chapter with the user, proceed paragraph by paragraph. Present one paragraph at a time, wait for the user's edits or approval, apply edits to that paragraph, then move to the next paragraph in the chapter.

Do not batch-review an entire chapter unless the user explicitly asks for a full-pass review. The default review method for this book is interactive paragraph-level editing.
