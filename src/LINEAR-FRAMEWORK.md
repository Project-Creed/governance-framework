# Governance Linear Framework

The Governance Framework moves from the foundations of public trust to the practices that make collective power legitimate, competent, constrained, accountable, and inheritable.

## Phase One: Establish Public Trust

The reader begins by recognizing that governance is not mere winning, ruling, managing, or protesting. It is public decision-making under reality, reciprocity, authority, constraint, and long-term responsibility.

Chapters: 01-06.

## Phase Two: Make Public Decisions

The reader then considers lawmaking, policy, taxation, budgeting, administration, and bureaucracy. Governance must connect public promises to evidence, costs, capacity, implementation, and review.

Chapters: 07-12.

## Phase Three: Preserve Political Accountability

The reader applies the framework to transparency, records, corruption, parties, elections, peaceful transfer, rights, duties, minority protection, and public goods. Political competition must remain bounded by truth, institutional trust, and the dignity of losing without losing membership.

Chapters: 13-18.

## Phase Four: Govern Power at Scale

The final movement concerns markets, civil society, emergencies, federal scale, foreign policy, treaties, technology, data, and the governed life. The test is whether public power remains responsible when consequences spread across borders, institutions, networks, and generations.

Chapters: 19-24.

## Operating Cadence

Use this cadence when applying the framework:

1. Name the public problem or decision.
2. Identify the evidence, tradeoffs, cost, capacity, and affected people.
3. Reverse roles among majority, minority, taxpayer, dependent, official, dissenter, and future citizen.
4. Identify who may decide and under what authority.
5. Name the rights, procedures, budgets, records, and review that constrain the decision.
6. Test the implementation incentives and administrative capacity.
7. Define accountability if the policy fails, is abused, or produces unintended harm.
8. Review what the pattern does to public trust over time.
